Higher Moments Of Observables in a Non-Linear Kerr Medium¶

Here the codes and the resulting plots for the observables and their combinations for a Kerr medium are given. The Hamiltonian for the system is given by:

$$ H = \hbar\chi(a^{\dagger 2} a^2) $$

where $a$ and $a^{\dagger}$ are the lowering and raising operators (conversely called as annihilation and creation operators when used in Quantum Optics), and they are given as, $$a = x + ip_x$$ $$a^{\dagger} = x - ip_x$$

We would like to recall that a certain superposition of states in a system, act as a eigen state to the lowering operator $a$, and we call this superposition state, the coherent state |$\psi_{\alpha}$> $$a |\alpha> = \alpha |\alpha>$$ where $\alpha$ is some complex number, a consequence of a being a non-hermitian eigen operator of the coherent state.

The time dependent coherent state $|\alpha (t)>$ is given as: $$|\alpha (t)> = e^{- \dfrac{|{\alpha^2}|}{2}} \sum_{n=0}^\infty \dfrac{\alpha^{n}}{\sqrt{n!}} e^{\dfrac{-iHt}{\hbar}}|n>$$

The general formula to find $<a>$ and $<a^{\dagger}>$ or any combination, $<a^{\dagger r} a^{r + s}>$ can be derived "easily" by the formula:

where $\nu = |\alpha|^2$,which we will henceforth forth refer to with the latter notation ($|\alpha|^2$) to avoid any confusion. We conveniently define $\alpha = \frac{p_1 + i q_1}{\sqrt{2}}$ , where $p_1$ and $q_1$ are some real numbers.

$$<a^{\dagger r} a^{r + s}> = \alpha^s \nu^r e^{-\nu (1 - cos2\chi st)} exp\{-i\chi (l(l - 1) + 2rs)t - i\nu sin2\chi st\} . . . . (1)$$

Here $\nu = |\alpha|^2$,which we will henceforth refer to with the latter notation ($|\alpha|^2$) to avoid any confusion. We conveniently define $\alpha = \frac{p_1 + i q_1}{\sqrt{2}}$ , which subsequently means that $|\alpha|^2 = \frac{(p_1^2 + q_1^2)}{2}$

Average of position as a function of time.¶

The average position with respect to time $<x(t)>$ can be obtained by noting that we get $<a>$ by setting r = 0 and s = 1 in (1), whereupon ,we get

$$<a(t)> = \alpha e^{- |\alpha|^2 (1 - cos2\chi t)} exp\{-i|\alpha|^2 sin2 \chi t\}$$
$$<x(t)> = e^{- |\alpha|^2 (1-cos(2\chi t)} [p_1 cos(|\alpha|^2 sin2\chi t)+q_1sin(|\alpha|^2 sin2\chi t)]$$

For higher moments of x and p.¶

Similarly, for higher moments of x and p, we would like to plot their values with respect to time, for which we can employ the above given formula. We find that $<x^2(t)>$ is:

$$<x^2(t)> = \frac{1}{2} \{1 + p_1^2 + q_1^2 + e^{-(p_1^2 + q_1^2)(1 - cos4\chi t)} [(p_1^2 - q_1^2)cos(2\chi t + (p^2_1 + q^2_1)sin4\chi t)+ 2p_1q_1sin(2\chi t+(p_1^2 + q_1^2)sin4\chi t]\}$$

It is worth noting here that, in addition to the periodicity associated with $2\chi t$ there is an additional periodicity in the form of $sin4\chi t$ within the sine and cosine terms. This leads to a curve that looks like:
